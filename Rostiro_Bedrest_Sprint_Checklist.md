## Daily template (repeat every day you're active)

**⚠️ Revised cadence (2026-07-16): account flagged "probably spam" on Day 2 after volume push. New-account trust > raw reps for the next 1-2 weeks.**
- [ ] **5-8 replies/day**, not 10-20 — ramp back up gradually as the account ages, don't front-load
- [ ] Spread replies across the *whole day* with real gaps, not two tight back-to-back sessions
- [ ] Vary phrasing/structure between replies — near-identical patterns across threads is a spam trigger
- [ ] Fill out profile completeness if not done — real photo, bio, phone verification all reduce spam-flag risk on a young account

**Morning (~15-20 min):**
- [ ] Glance Lists 1 + 2
- [ ] Check X Trending for overnight breaks
- [ ] Reply session 1 (aim ~half of today's target)
- [ ] Check Discord `#mentions` for Mention Radar hits, reply on-platform to genuine ones

**Anytime:**
- [ ] 1 original X post (2 only on genuinely major news) — pull from the calendar below, tweak freely
- [ ] 2-3 warm-network DMs from the table above
- [ ] Nothing real to post? Skip it. A weak post is worse than silence.

**Evening (~15-20 min):**
- [ ] Reply session 2 (close out today's target)
- [ ] List 3 check (a few times/week is enough, not daily)

**Bed-time capacity — redirect the extra hours here instead of reply volume:**
- [ ] Warm-network DMs (this scales safely — no platform risk, and it's the highest-leverage bed task anyway)
- [ ] One Reddit/Discord value-first comment in r/fantasyfootball, r/DynastyFF, r/FFCommish, or a league Discord — no brand mention, karma-building only (new Reddit account, same trust-gate issue)

**Rest days:** take up to 2/week where posting drops to 0-1 and replies drop to a handful. A rest day isn't falling behind — burning out mid-sprint is the actual risk.
