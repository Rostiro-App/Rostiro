## 1. The decision: X is the only channel we actively grow through Sept 9

**Why:** fantasy football's real community — the writers, the takes, the trade arguments, the people who'd actually try a new tool — lives on X, and it ramps up hard through August. IG/TikTok require a video pipeline that doesn't exist yet (Simulation Studio has never run a session), and splitting a solo founder's limited time across three cold channels means none of them compound. Go deep on one before going wide.

**What this changes vs. the original plan:**
- IG/TikTok/YouTube (the "video trio") are **paused, not abandoned.** Stay connected in Postiz; don't actively push content or chase the Studio-session unlock as a near-term priority. Revisit once X has real traction to learn from.
- The planned IG carousel (breaking-news + player-intelligence + about-card) is **shelved.** Mixing "here's NFL news" content with "here's our product" content on the same feed reads as unfocused, and it's not where the effort should go right now anyway.
- LinkedIn and Reddit stay exactly as before — manual, permanently, trust channels where a human posting is the signal (see the Appendix for the full original rationale, still valid).
- **The single biggest addition: replying, not just posting, is now the primary growth tactic.** See §3.

## 2. Daily cadence

This is the concrete answer to "how many times a day":

| Activity | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Original posts on X** | **1/day baseline, up to 2 on a genuinely big news day** (a real blockbuster trade, a major injury with a real fantasy angle) | Never more than 2. Skip the day entirely if there's nothing real to say — a weak post posted just to hit a number is worse than silence. |
| **Replies on X** | **10–15/day, every day** (founder-set, 2026-07-15 — higher than the original 8–12 recommendation) | The actual growth lever. Spread across 2 short sessions (e.g. ~15 min morning, ~15 min evening) rather than one batch — sustained activity through the day earns more distribution than a single burst. |
| **Rest days** | Up to 2/week where posting drops to 0–1 and replies drop to a handful | Solo founder also building the product — sustainable beats burned out. Don't let a rest day become a rest week. If 10-15/day ever starts feeling unsustainable next to running the product, dial back rather than grind through it — the number is a target, not an obligation. |

**Why replies matter this much:** at zero followers, a post into your own feed is close to invisible — there's no existing audience for the algorithm to show it to. A good reply on someone else's post with real reach is how people actually discover a new account. This is the highest-leverage thing that was missing from the original plan.

**Discovery layer, updated 2026-07-15:** following ~100–200 NFL-related accounts broadly (not just the curated Lists) as a second discovery net on top of §9's 3 Lists — accepted tradeoff: follow ratio will look skewed for a while, worth it for reply-opportunity coverage. **The 3 Lists stay the fast daily glance** (curated, high-signal, 2-3 minutes); **the broad-follow timeline is the backup scan** for anything genuinely viral breaking outside the curated set. Turn on post notifications for the highest-value accounts (the ones most likely to break something worth being early on) so you're not relying on catching it in either feed — being one of the first replies on a viral post matters more than being a good reply late.

## 3. The reply playbook

1. **Build and maintain a target list of ~20–30 accounts** — fantasy analysts, bigger fantasy personality/meme accounts, and a few other indie-builder/build-in-public accounts. Check their recent posts daily.
2. **Every reply should be genuinely sharp, funny, or a real contrarian take** — not "great post!" filler, and not a disguised product plug. The goal is to be worth reading, not to sell anything. If Rostiro comes up naturally because of a genuine State-system insight, fine; forcing it in is not.
3. **Replies are Lawrence's, not Jarvis's, to write and send.** This is deliberate: an authentic voice replying in real threads is exactly the kind of thing that shouldn't be automated — see the honesty contract and the humor-customization design elsewhere in this system. Jarvis's role here is limited to §6 below (surfacing opportunities on request), never posting a reply itself.

## 4. Content pillars (what the 1/day original post actually is)

The account can't out-report ESPN/PFF/Yahoo on volume or speed, and shouldn't try — that's the trap the old "News Desk" framing risked. Every post should filter through what's actually distinct about Rostiro (the State system, the cross-league view, "here's what your week looks like") rather than being generic NFL news with a logo on it. Rotate through these, don't force a strict daily schedule:

- **Product-lens take** — a real news item or fantasy debate, reframed through something only Rostiro's worldview would notice (e.g. how a trade shifts a player's State, not just "he got traded").
- **Founder / build-in-public** — real progress, a real screenshot, a real decision explained honestly. People follow a builder before they follow a brand at zero followers.
- **Hot take / personality** — the founder's own humor and voice, genuinely funny over generically clever. This is a real strength per §0 of this doc's premise — use it.
- **Community question** — something that invites replies (a debate prompt, "who's your Week 1 sleeper," etc.) — these tend to earn more distribution than a pure broadcast post.

**Two audience segments, not one** (confirmed via the Reddit expansion in §5, worth carrying into X content too): the veteran running 5+ leagues who wants everything in one place, AND a newer player who just wants to feel less lost. An occasional beginner-friendly pillar post ("here's what actually matters in Week 1 waivers if this is your first year") is fair game alongside the power-user framing — don't let every post assume the reader already runs multiple leagues.

This is preseason (dynasty startup drafts, way-too-early rankings, ADP debates are the actual live conversation right now) — lean into that calendar, not the in-season news cycle that doesn't exist yet.

## 5. Community seeding (secondary track, parallel to X)

Fantasy football is inherently viral within a league — 8–12 friends who already talk constantly. This is likely faster than growing a cold X following in 8 weeks, and runs in parallel, not instead:
- r/fantasyfootball, r/DynastyFF, r/FFCommish, r/SleeperApp — value-first comments only, same rule as Reddit always had (see the Appendix). Genuine answers in real threads, never a templated drop.
- Fantasy football Discord servers — same value-first rule.
- Direct: get the product into a few real leagues this preseason, including Lawrence's own.

**Discovery here IS automated — the actual reply/posting is not.** The real n8n workflow **"Rostiro - Mention Radar"** (`automations/n8n/README.md` Workflow 7, live, polls every 30 min) searches Reddit for brand mentions + pain-phrases across those 4 subreddits, has Claude Haiku judge each candidate ENGAGE/SKIP, and pings Discord `#mentions` with a short nudge on genuine hits — it never posts anything itself, Lawrence always replies in his own words on-platform.

**Expanded 2026-07-15** to catch two audience segments instead of one:
1. **Multi-league power users** (original scope) — "multiple leagues," "manage all my," "run every league," "ten tabs."
2. **NEW — general tool discovery**: "fantasy football app," "fantasy football tool" — someone asking what to use, not necessarily complaining about multiple leagues specifically.
3. **NEW — newbie confidence**: "new to fantasy football," "first time fantasy football," "fantasy football beginner" — a second real audience Rostiro can serve (not just the veteran running 5 leagues, but someone nervous about their first one). The judgment prompt was updated to match: for a genuine beginner post, being a helpful fantasy player first is often the whole value — it will ENGAGE without necessarily mentioning Rostiro at all if that's not genuinely on-topic, same honesty-contract discipline as the original scope.

This track stays reply-manual — no automation posts anything, no Jarvis involvement beyond drafting a Reddit comment on request (existing manual-only policy, unchanged) — but discovery is now hands-free for both segments instead of requiring Lawrence to go looking.

## 10. Season kickoff ramp — Week 1, Sept 9 onward

**The premise, confirmed by the founder 2026-07-14: the real growth window is the first few weeks of the season, not the preseason build-up.** Everything above through Labor Day is about being credible and present *before* the window opens — the actual surge in fantasy football attention (and therefore the actual opportunity) hits hard starting Week 1. Plan for a step change in intensity, not a continuation of the preseason pace.

**What changes when the season starts:**
- **Reply volume goes up, not down** — game windows (Thu night, Sun day/night, Mon night) are when fantasy Twitter is at its most active all week. Treat each game window as a mini reply-sprint: check List 1 + List 2 during the game itself, not just once in the morning.
- **Original posts can exceed the 1-2/day ceiling on Sundays specifically** — live scoring chaos, waiver-priority calls, and in-game reactions are genuinely time-sensitive in a way preseason takes aren't. The quality bar (§0 standing directive) still applies — more posts, never weaker ones.
- **Content shifts from "building trust" to "being useful in the moment"** — a real-time "who to start" call, a live injury reaction with the fantasy angle, a Sunday-morning "your lineup by 1pm" post. This is where Rostiro's actual product value (the State system reacting to Game Day) becomes the most natural, honest thing to post about — not a stretch, the real thing.
- **Pre-build a few evergreen assets before Week 1 hits** so you're not writing from scratch mid-chaos: a "how Pulse works on Sunday" explainer post, a couple of stat-card templates pre-filled with placeholder structure ready for real numbers, a pinned-tweet-worthy "what Rostiro is" post for the follower surge.
- **Watch analytics weekly, not just for pillar mix (§7) but for capacity** — if Sunday reply volume is unsustainable solo, that's a real signal to simplify rather than burn out; the plan should flex to what's actually sustainable once real volume hits, not stay rigid to a preseason estimate.

**Between now and Sept 9:** the preseason weeks (§2-§8 above) are explicitly the ramp-up — building List 1/2/3 familiarity, finding your voice and pillar mix, getting comfortable with the reply cadence — so that Week 1's step-change in intensity is executing a practiced motion, not starting cold.
